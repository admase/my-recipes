Poke Dish

Description

Poke is a Hawaiian dish, its name derives from the Hawaiian word "to slice or cut." The name is a good indicator of what the dish is because poke is traditionally sliced raw fish (usually tuna or octopus) that's marinated in a savory, umami-rich sauce made with soy sauce, sesame oil, and green onions.

Incredients
  • 2 pounds fresh Ahi Tuna, cubed
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoon finely chopped macadamia nuts
  • 1 tablespoon crushed red peppers (Optional)
Steps: To Make Poke
  1. Making poke is so incredibly easy. Simply combine all the ingredients in a bowl and allow it to marinate in the fridge for at least two hours. Serve as desired
